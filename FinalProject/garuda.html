Garuda

Abilities:
  • Stormbringer.

Philippines. in Luzon Mythology, Garuda or Galurâ (in Kapampangan) is a winged assistant of Apúng Sinukuan (Mariang Sinukuan), he is represented by a giant eagle and believed to be the bringer of storms. The Maranao people of southern Philippines believe in a race of creatures called garuda who dwell beneath the sea.

Story:
In Hinduism, Garuda is described as the king of birds and a kite-like figure. He is shown either in zoomorphic form (giant bird with partially open wings) or an anthropomorphic form (man with wings and some bird features). Garuda is generally a protector with power to swiftly go anywhere, ever watchful and an enemy of the serpent. The kite/bird motif appears in versions of both the Tagalog and Visayan creation myths. The image of Garuda has also appeared in 10th-13th century gold-work from the Eastern Visayas and Agusan del Norte. In some Maranao folk beliefs, Garuda is a winged monster with big teeth and huge talons that can carry six men. It lives under the sea and on occasion decimates the countryside with raids, devouring all in its path.
